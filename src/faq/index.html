Flights

You'll want to fly into Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL). Google Flights will likely be your best bet for exploring flight options. There are a few airlines you'll be able to choose from.

Hot tip! Seats in front of the bulkhead will have more leg room.

Turkish Airlines

A great option if you've got some wiggle room in your dates. Turkish Airlines provides Touristanbul, a free layover service for all international transfer through Istanbul. If you fly via Istanbul and have a 6-24 hour layover time, they'll provide transportation to and from historic sites & restaurants in Istanbul. A pretty cool way to get some sightseeing in one of Aparajita's favourite cities in the world!

Ethiopian Airlines

A good choice if you're interesting in bumping up to business class -- they tend to have good business class fares.

Air Canada/Air India

These two airlines operate codeshare flights and frequently provide direct flights to Delhi from Toronto, Montreal, or Vancouver. The direct flights are long (16 hours) but will get you there fastest. They'll typically span 7AM Canada time to 11PM India time which can be helpful in managing jet lag.

Egypt Air

Similarly to Turkish Airlines, Egypt Air also provides sightseeing day trips if your layover allows it.

Visas/Travel Documents

eVisa

Canadian passport holders need a visa to visit India. We recommend applying for the 1- or 5-year multiple entry visa. These will allow you to leave and return (in case you're traveling to countries outside of India during your trip) and can be applied for up to 120 days before your trip. While there is a 30-day visa, it can only be applied for 30 days in advance of travel and it's single entry only.

eVisas can be applied for online (via this link) and have had a rapid turnaround time (~1 day) for guests who've already applied. There's no guarantee this will remain the case however so apply as early as you can!

Vaccinations

You may need some vaccines to travel to India (or other destinations you're planning to visit). We recommend booking a consultation with a local travel immunization clinic: they'll tell you what vaccines are recommended based on your intended destinations. Start this process early however since some vaccinations may need multiple doses and travel clinics can sometimes have a bit of a backlog.

Indian Dress

Here's a quick overview of the basic types of Indian outfits you could wear. Indian wear isn't mandatory but it is encouraged!

  1. {% imageCard "/assets/img/kurta_men.jpg" "Kurta (Men)" "left" %}

    A long, dress-like top worn with big, flowy pants that snug up around the calves. For men, this will pretty much be your bread and butter -- Kurta styles range from the day-to-day casual to more formal or extravagant.

    {% endimageCard %}
  2. {% imageCard "/assets/img/kurta_women.jpg" "Kurta (Women)" "right" %}

    Similar to the basic kurtas worn by men. Women will often wear kurtas with a chunni (a light scarf). They'll also typically have a wider selection of pant types -- loose, snug, flared, etc.

    {% endimageCard %}
  3. {% imageCard "/assets/img/lehnga.jpg" "Lehnga" "left" %}

    A large skirt paired with a short top and worn with a chunni (a light scarf). This ladies' outfit is comfy and easy to wear, making it a good choice for nights with dancing.

    {% endimageCard %}
  4. {% imageCard "/assets/img/saree.jpg" "Saree" "right" %}

    A long piece of cloth you wrap around yourself, with a short blouse and petticoat underneath. Sarees can be a bit particular to wrap correctly and they'd be tough to manage on the dance floor, but don't let that dissuade you! There are tons of people who'd love nothing more than to show you the ropes.

Buying Indian Clothes

Malls

All the big malls in Delhi will have stores where you can buy off the shelf sizes. Common retailers include:

  • Pantaloons
  • Global Desi
  • Biba's
  • Fab India (one of the more authentic/old school options)
  • Lifestyle
  • Kilol
  • Anhoki

Markets

The street markets of Delhi are great for both sightseeing and finding clothing, souvenirs, etc. These are the markets we'd recommend for your visit.

  • Dilli Haat -- local artisan market in Delhi where you can find handmade items
  • Janpath -- street shopping in Delhi where you can find both Indian and Western clothes, and lots of other handcrafts
  • Chandni Chowk -- massive wholesale market in Old Delhi
    • Well connected by metro but no cars allowed
    • Can be a bit difficult to navigate without guidance as it is both huge and crowded
    • A fun experience just to visit, but don't bank on finding what you need if you don't know exactly where to go

AirBnB

AirBnBs in Delhi are a good option, especially if you're looking to share accommodations with other guests. Expect to pay around $100-$300 CAD per night for 3-4 bedroom houses.

Near central Delhi, the neighbourhoods we'd recommend are:

  • Connaught Place
  • Bengali Market
  • Gol Market
  • Mandi House

In South Delhi, you'll find some artsier neighbourhoods. The ones we'd recommend there are:

  • Haus Kauz
  • Greater Kailash

If you have other neighbourhoods you're interested in, feel free to reach out to us to get the scoop on them.

Hotels

There are a ton of hotel options in Delhi. These will typically be more expensive than AirBnBs but you'll have a higher guarantee for a comfortable stay. Expect to pay around $200 CAD per night per room for the nicer hotels. We've had good experiences with the following hotels in central Delhi:

  • Park Hotel
  • Imperial Hotel

Again -- if you're looking at other hotels and have any questions, feel free to send them our way.

Transportation

Within Delhi

You've got a couple options for mobility within the city.

Rideshare

Rideshare apps (Uber, Ola, Lyft) are all widely available, take credit cards, and are fairly cheap.

Metro

The metro system of Delhi is very well connected but can be a bit crowded, especially at peak imes.

Car Rental

If you're looking at renting a car, rent one with a driver. It will be hard to find a rental car without a driver and you probably don't want to drive in Delhi anyways. Expect to pay about $50 CAD per day for the rental & driver.

Trains

India has one of the oldest and most well connected train systems. It's worth taking a trip just for the experience! Bookings can be made online and in advance (tickets do tend to fill up, so don't wait too long). First or second class bookings are not much more expensive and are well worth the additional comfort.

Cars

Rental cars (with drivers) are also a good, cheap, and convenient option. 3-4 hour drive trips to e.g., Agra, Jaipur are a great candidate for a 1 or 2 day rental.

Flights

You'll be able to find cheap, well connected flights to all major cities from Delhi. Booking well in advance will get you the best rates.
